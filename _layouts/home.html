I'm an undergrad at Yale studying math and computer science. Most of my time goes toward understanding why machine learning models work — or more often, why they don't. I think about out-of-distribution detection, multi-task learning, and lately, what happens inside neural networks when you look at them through the lens of statistics.

I've been lucky to present this work at CVPR, NeurIPS, and AAAI, and before college I was the youngest speaker at the Databricks Data+AI Summit. When I'm not doing research I build things people actually use — a machine learning library, Chrome extensions, accessibility tools. I also photograph birds.

This site is where I write about the ideas I find most interesting — Bayesian inference, learning theory, stochastic calculus — and share the notes and derivations I write for myself along the way.
