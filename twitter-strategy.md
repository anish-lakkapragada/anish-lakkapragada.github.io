# Twitter Launch Strategy — anishlk.com

## Timing

**Best time to post:** Tuesday 10–11am ET (7–8am PT). Tech/ML Twitter is most active mid-morning Eastern. Avoid posting after 3pm ET — engagement drops off sharply.

**Day:** Tuesday is ideal. Monday people are catching up on email. Wednesday+ you're competing with mid-week fatigue. Tuesday gives you the full week for the post to circulate.

## The Launch Tweet

Your launch tweet should be a **thread opener**. The first tweet is the hook — it needs to work standalone in someone's timeline. Don't lead with "I made a website." Lead with *why someone should care*.

### Option A: Lead with credibility, reveal the site

> I'm an undergrad at Yale studying CS + Math.
>
> I've published at CVPR, NeurIPS, and AAAI. Built Chrome extensions used by 30K+ people. Written 14 sets of LaTeX notes on measure theory, stochastic calculus, and learning theory — all free.
>
> I finally made a website that reflects all of this: anishlk.com

### Option B: Lead with the content, not yourself

> I've been writing up math and ML derivations for the past two years — proofs of the optional stopping theorem, Black-Scholes under risk-neutral measure, PAC-Bayes bounds, VC dimension, and more.
>
> 14 sets of LaTeX notes. 8 long-form blog posts. All free on my new site: anishlk.com

### Option C: Lead with a specific impressive thing

> I built a sparse autoencoder that runs live in your browser with TensorFlow.js.
>
> It's on my new personal site, alongside 5 research papers (CVPR, NeurIPS, AAAI), 14 LaTeX note sets, and 8 blog posts on Bayesian inference, learning theory, and stochastic calculus.
>
> anishlk.com

## What to Do After Posting

1. **Pin the thread** to your profile immediately
2. **Like and reply** to every response within the first 2 hours — early engagement feeds the algorithm
3. **Quote-tweet your own thread** the next day with a different angle (e.g., highlight a specific blog post or note)
4. **Engage with ML Twitter** the day before and day of — reply thoughtfully to accounts in your space so your name is visible when people see the launch tweet

## Who to Tag (Sparingly)

Don't @-mention people in the launch tweet — it looks desperate. Instead:
- **Quote tweet** or reply to relevant ML/math accounts in the days before
- If someone's work directly influenced your blog posts (e.g., the Transformer Circuits team for SAE work), a genuine reply or quote-tweet is appropriate
- If you know any Yale/ML mutuals, DM them the link and ask if they'd RT

## Profile Setup Before Launch

- **Bio** should match the site's signal: "Yale CS + Math. Research at CVPR/NeurIPS/AAAI. Writing about statistics, ML theory, and building things. anishlk.com"
- **Pinned tweet** should be the launch thread
- **Header image**: consider a screenshot of the SAE demo or a clean crop of one of your derivations
- **Profile picture**: use the same photo as your site for brand consistency

## After Launch Week

- Share individual blog posts as standalone tweets, not just links — pull out one interesting result or visualization and make it the tweet, with the link at the end
- Your notes are extremely shareable — "here's a free proof of the optional stopping theorem for martingales" is the kind of thing that gets bookmarked
- The Unsplash bird photography angle (6.3M views) is a great humanizing contrast piece — consider a separate tweet about it sometime in the week after

## What NOT to Do

- Don't say "I finally got around to making a website" — that undersells it
- Don't apologize for anything on the site
- Don't ask "what do you think?" — state what it is confidently
- Don't launch on a weekend — engagement is significantly lower
- Don't post and disappear — the first 2 hours of engagement are critical
