I'm an undergrad at Yale studying math and computer science. Most of my research is about understanding machine learning through the lens of statistics — figuring out when and why models generalize, fail, or behave unexpectedly. I've worked on [out-of-distribution detection](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2401.10176), [multi-task learning](https://arxiv.org/abs/2211.12999), and more recently I've been thinking about [phase transitions](https://arxiv.org/abs/2401.00186) in neural network training through Singular Learning Theory.

Before Yale, I was a researcher at [Stanford's Wall Lab](https://wall-lab.stanford.edu/) building ML systems for early autism detection, and later at the [U.S. Air Force Research Lab](https://www.afrl.af.mil/) (where I was the youngest intern they'd had — the position was typically held by undergrad and grad students). I was also the youngest-ever speaker at Databricks' Data+AI Summit.

I love building software, especially things that reach a lot of people. I wrote [SeaLion](https://github.com/anish-lakkapragada/SeaLion), a machine learning library from scratch in high school that got 300+ stars on GitHub. At [alphaXiv](https://alphaxiv.org) I ideated and shipped a Chrome extension that now has 30,000+ users. I also built [GenAlt](https://chromewebstore.google.com/detail/genalt-generate-ai-altern/ekbmkapnmnhhgfmjdnchgmcfggibebnn), an accessibility tool for the visually impaired that was featured at the U.S. Interagency Accessibility Forum, and a dining hall analytics extension for [Yale](https://chromewebstore.google.com/detail/yale-hospitality-wrapped/idodopjdnfehepljegmmmkgbelpogple) and [USC](https://dailytrojan.com/2025/02/20/students-launch-usc-hospitality-wrapped/) (the USC one was covered by the Daily Trojan).

Outside of work, I [photograph birds](https://unsplash.com/@anishlk) — my photos have 6.3 million views and 30,000+ downloads on Unsplash. I also read a lot of [technical](https://suzyahyah.github.io) [blogs](https://jiha-kim.github.io) and play poker.
